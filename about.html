About

History

Zivai Community Empowerment Trust (ZICET) is a Community Based Organisation (CBO) that was established in 2015 by women and youths in Mutare and Mutasa Rural District after realising that most women and youths are being left out in the economic development agenda. Women and Youth exclusion was noted as being most pronounced in the mining sector which is an important pillar to the economic development of Zimbabwe. During its conception, the organisation noted that Women and youths have limited opportunities for employment in the mining sector. If opportunities are extended in the mining sector for these marginalised groups, there will be realisation of Sustainable development Goal 5, which is important in the Government’s achievement of its overall vision on equity and equality.

ZICET was formed on the bases of challenges faced and lessons learnt in diamond mining in Chiadzwa and Marange. The organisation took those lessons and decided to focus on gold mining in Penhalonga to improve the environment under which mining was taking place by establishing an organization that seek to address the inequality and limited participation of women and youth in mining industry.

At its inception, ZICET sought to advance the protection of natural resources through training of communities and miners on good environmental conservation practices. ZICET’s focus considered how most young women in rural areas of Mutare and Mutasa continue to be marginalised due to patriarchal society and different religious norms. It’s programming focus also considered the intersection between mining and gender-based violence which has prompted ZICET to focus on gender mainstreaming and peace building programs in Mining communities. Societies where natural resources exist bemoan the lack of transparency and accountability in processes of extraction, utilisation and revenue sharing and this informed the organisation to invest in resource governance at local level. ZICET therefore seek to advocate for the improvement of natural resources governance and environmental management by working hand in glove with important stakeholders like Ministry of Mines, Ministry of Environment, and other important stakeholders.

Our Vision

Youth, Women and People with Disabilities who Prosper and Enjoy their Rights to Natural Resources. We believe access to information on resources and benefits that accrue from public resources as well as information on development plans is crucial for every citizen to access. We strongly seek for natural resources integrity to spur development of mining host communities.

Our work

Our Mission

To provide and create a platform for women and youth to identify and utilise their potential for the meaningful development of their local areas through community based natural resources management. We seek to promote equitable and inclusive participation of marginalised communities in attaining social, economic, and environmental just development.
